Demo notes (would not ship in production):
• Cluster 4 (Dark Forest-Green Botanical Product Showcase) chrome applied to pdx-gardener.
• User-content protection strategy: dark forest page surface NEVER touches user content directly. User photos + product names + owner names all live inside cream cards floating on the dark surface. This is the structural answer to "how do user-uploaded photos survive a dark-mode app".
• Color semantic remap: free/paid/barter tag colors retuned for cream-card surface (not page) so they stay readable while preserving semantic meaning (free=green, paid=warm-red, barter=warm-yellow). All ≥ WCAG AA contrast.
• Gold (#C9A75A) used sparingly as accent: eyebrow uppercase Latin, dividers, section count, link borders. Editorial discipline: max one gold accent per visual chunk.
• Brand-promise tension acknowledged: this language pulls pdx-gardener away from "neighborhood Saturday community board" toward "Pacific NW forest at dusk / botanical curated showcase". The community-board owner should decide whether that elevation is desirable.
• Risks: (1) elderly-user readability of cream-on-dark page chrome may decrease vs current; (2) emoji icons (🥬/📋/🌱) feel cheap against gold-rule editorial chrome — would need custom monoline botanical icons in a real ship.
• Layout invariants preserved: ≥44×44 touch targets, flex-row 80×80 thumb cards, color-semantic tags, section ordering.
